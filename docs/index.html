Digitization of Course Syllabi in Blekinge Institute of Technology since the 1990s

The digitization of course syllabi   in Blekinge Institute of Technology since the 1990s involve the conversion of paper-based syllabi into digital formats, making it easier for students and faculty to access and use them remotely.

The project's main purpose is to provide digital access to the course syllabi. Despite being available only in hard copy, former students frequently request access to these course syllabi to verify their education when applying for new programs.
Additional purposes of this project include preserving the historical record of the course syllabi and making them easily accessible to current and future students, faculty, and researchers at Blekinge Institute of Technology.

In this project, nine different course syllabi from the humanities program were selected based on their development within a specific field over time.

It is also worth noting that, these course syllabi are not copyright protected and are considered public records, in results, making the digital reproductions openly available on the web.
